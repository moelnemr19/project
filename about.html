Socila Media About Messi:

Lionel Andres Messi was born on June 24, 1987 in Rosario, Argentina and began playing football since his childhood. In 1995, he joined the youth team at Newell's Old Boys (a first-division football club based in Rosario). According to the British Encyclopedia, Messi's incredible skills caught the attention of prestigious clubs on both sides of the Atlantic. At the age of 13, he and his family moved to Barcelona where he began playing with the Barcelona under-14 team. He scored 21 goals in 14 matches for the youth team and quickly advanced to the first team. Messi participated in his first unofficial match with Barcelona when he was 16 years old, In the 2004-2005 season, Messi, who was 17 years old at the time, became the youngest player and top scorer in the Spanish league. Despite his humble physique, with a height of 1.7 meters and a weight of 67 kilograms, he was strong, balanced, and versatile on the field. He was fast, accurate, played with his left foot, distributed passes, and could easily penetrate through solid defenses, In 2005, Messi obtained Spanish citizenship, and the following year he and Barcelona won the UEFA Champions League (European club championship). The Argentine star played 778 matches with the Catalan team, a record that no other player in the club's history has achieved. Messi scored 672 goals with Barcelona, and won 10 Spanish league titles and 4 UEFA Champions League titles In addition, he has won the Ballon d'Or award as the best football player in the world 6 times Messi's first international appearance was in 2005 as a substitute against Hungary, where he played for two minutes before being sent off. However, he quickly proved his worth in the national team and traveled to Germany to participate in the 2006 World Cup. He also participated in the 2010 World Cup in South Africa, the 2014 World Cup in Brazil where Argentina reached the final, and the 2018 World Cup in Russia. In 2021, Messi wanted to apply for a free transfer, but Barcelona refused and said that whoever wants to buy Messi would have to pay 700 million euros (624 million pounds sterling). However, those restrictions have now been lifted. According to the Argentine newspaper "Clarín" in a headline titled "Global Shock...Will Paris Saint-Germain be his new club?", the French club was considered "the main candidate to add him to its stars" such as Kylian Mbappe and Neymar. The newspaper added that "Messi is at the peak of his performance and with his eyes on the Qatar World Cup, he will continue to bet on European football", pointing out that he had been flirting with Paris Saint-Germain, which is owned by Qatar, for many years. Some Argentine teams have also started dreaming of getting his services, although the economic reality in this South American country, which is experiencing a severe recession, may make his return to his homeland a more surprising event. The hashtag "Welcome Leo" began to spread on Twitter, with pictures of Messi wearing the shirts of prestigious Argentine clubs such as Boca Juniors, River Plate, Newell's Old Boys, and San Lorenzo, as well as more humble clubs like Sarmiento. After much speculation, Lionel Messi joined Paris Saint-Germain. The Argentine star, who joined the City of Lights club on a free transfer deal, is set to earn 35 million euros annually, and this amount will not be subject to taxes, in addition to incentives and other variables, according to European media reports. It is worth mentioning that Messi is married to the fashion model Antonella Roccuzzo, and they have three children together, named Thiago, Mateo, and Tio.
